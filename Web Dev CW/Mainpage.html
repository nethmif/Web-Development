Culture in Sri Lanka



Cultural destinations in Sri Lanka

The Sri Lankan government directed the use of cultural heritages to uplift tourism with the introduction of the central cultural fund in 1980, and the cultural triangle was formed based on old and ancient places such as Anuradhapura, Polonnaruwa, and Kandy. The Cultural Triangle's world heritages can be divided into the following categories:

1) Alive religious cities (Anuradhapura, Polonnaruwa)
2) Historical old cities (Polonnaruwa, Sigiriya)
3) Old cave temples ( Rangiri Dambulla)

Adventure in Sri Lanka



Adventurous places in Sri Lanka

Sri Lanka is a country with a variety of adventurous activities and breathtaking landscapes to explore.There are many exciting places to visit for adventure seekers, ranging from ancient rock fortresses to scenic national parks.
Some popular hiking destinations in the central highlands are Ella Rock, Knuckles Mountain Range, and Horton Plains National Park.Arugam Bay,Nilaveli are famous surfing destinations in the world, with world-class waves attracting surfers from all over the world.
Overall, Sri Lanka is a destination that provides a diverse range of adventurous activities and experiences, such as hiking and surfing, as well as wildlife safaris.

Leisure in Sri Lanka



Destinations of entertainment

Sri Lanka provides a wide range of entertainment options for visitors, including nightlife, cultural experiences, outdoor activities, and shopping.
Colombo's vibrant nightlife and cultural attractions are well-known, and cultural shows provide insight into the country's rich history and heritage.
Sri Lanka's beaches provide outdoor entertainment options, and the country is also a great shopping destination, with markets, malls, and street vendors selling a wide range of goods.
Overall, Sri Lanka has something for everyone who is looking for entertainment and leisure activities.

Agriculture in Sri Lanka



Farming Areas in Sri Lanka

Sri Lanka has a strong agricultural past, with a wide variety of crops growing all around the country. Tea plantations are prominent tourist attractions in the central highlands, while rubber plantations can be found in places like Haputale and Kegalle.
Other notable crops farmed in Sri Lanka include coconut, spices, and rice, with facilities like the Coconut Research Institute in Lunuwila and the spice gardens in Matale providing opportunity to learn about agricultural processes and sample a range of spices.
Generally, visitors interested in learning about the country's crops and cultivation processes can enjoy a variety of agricultural experiences in Sri Lanka.
